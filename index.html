Michelin North America

Greenville, South Carolina, USA
Software Engineer, End User Experience

May 2018 - August 2018

At Michelin my work revolved around expediting services and resources to Michelin employees. Using the Microsoft Bot Frame, I developed two chatbots for our internal communication platform (Microsoft Teams). The objective of my project was to enhance our Microsoft Teams platform to centralize all our information for Michelin employees and contractors; making information easy to find to help employees spend less time searching, and more time doing.

Using the Service Now REST API, I developed a chatbot to retrieve knowledge base articles to provide a more accessible way to search through a plethora of articles. The articles were previously available through the company intranet; this limited access to the articles and were not available via mobile devices or outside of the company intranet. In addition to enhancing the Microsoft Teams platform, it also solved the limitations of accessing the articles through the company intranet.

Additionally, I created another chatbot to search through Michelin acronyms and abbreviations. As a long-established company, Michelin, has hundreds of hundreds of acronyms and abbreviations. On top of the hundreds of acronyms, Michelin is a French company, which adds another hundred acronyms, in French. To aid daily communication between teams and employees, I developed another chatbot to search through acronyms and retrieve the meaning of such acronyms.

Expediting information was another big part of my time at Michelin, I aided in automating part of the employee reimbursement and transaction process. Initially a Michelin employee would fill out a reimbursement form, include the pertinent receipts, and send it for review. One employee would review the forms and receipts and then create a folder on a shared One Drive folder, which another employee would have to check consistently to then create a transaction entry in Clarity to then process the transaction. I automated the process by creating a script that would detect when a new folder was created to then create a card in the team's Kanban board; this sped the process by removing the reliance on an employee having to check the One Drive folder to create the transaction entry.
